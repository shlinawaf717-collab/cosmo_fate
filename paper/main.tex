\title{Is cosmic fate an inference or an assumption?\\
A preregistered fragility audit of the heat-death conclusion\\
with DESI DR2, Pantheon+, and \textit{Planck}}

\author{Jiacheng Zhang}
\email{shlinawaf717@gmail.com}
\affiliation{Independent researcher}

\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
The DESI era has produced a wave of claims about the ultimate fate of the
Universe. We ask a different question: how much of the fate verdict is forced
by the data, and how much is donated by the assumptions? Under a preregistered
plan (frozen before real-data fate analysis; three logged amendments), we
combine DESI DR2 BAO, \textit{Planck} 2018 distance priors, and
Pantheon+/SH0ES supernovae, propagate every posterior sample to $a=10^4$
through a frozen fate classifier, and differentiate the verdict along eight
assumption axes. Baseline (CPL): $P(\textsc{decay})=99.8\%$,
$P(\textsc{rip})=0.18\%$ (independently verified by nested sampling). Null
calibration on one hundred mock universes with exact-\lcdm{} truth shows the
\emph{direction} of such verdicts is a coin flip---heat-death-compatible mass
distributed as $U(0,1)$, $52/48$ even at zero noise---while the \emph{depth}
of the real data's anti-rip preference is genuine (calibrated $p<0.030$,
converging with Wilks $p=0.024$). The translation into fate is
assumption-dominated: swapping supernova compilations moves the \lcdm{}
exclusion from $2.25\sigma$ to $3.27\sigma$; a quintessence prior sets
$P(\textsc{rip})\equiv0$ at a fit cost of $\Delta\chi^2=8.9$; Bayesian
evidence \emph{favors} \lcdm{} ($\ln B=-1.76$) while $\Delta\chi^2$ disfavors
it; and across five $w(a)$ grammars indistinguishable inside the data window,
the verdict runs from $P(\textsc{ds})=100\%$ (donated exactly by a
Gaussian-process mean function) through near-certain \textsc{decay} (CPL) to
a three-way coin flip (binned $w$): $P(\textsc{rip})$ spans $0$--$41\%$,
heat-death compatibility $58.8$--$100\%$, and the binned grammar returns a
measured $w(z{<}0.3)=-1.000\pm0.044$---the knife edge itself. Data information
terminates at $a=1$; every eschatological bit beyond it is
grammar-transported. We conclude that current fate claims report the echo of
their extrapolation conventions, and we release the audit framework and
pipeline for reuse.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

The DESI era has revived the oldest question in cosmology. Baryon acoustic
oscillation measurements from DESI DR2 \cite{DESIDR2}, combined with cosmic
microwave background (CMB) data and any of the current Type Ia supernova
compilations, prefer a dark-energy equation of state that was recently below
$-1$ and is now rising: the ``thawing'' quadrant $\wz>-1$, $\wa<0$. The
statistical strength of this preference is famously unstable---between
$2.25\sigma$ and $4.2\sigma$ depending on which supernova compilation and
which CMB likelihood one adopts
\cite{DESIDR2,Dovekie2025,CompressedEvidence2025,Efstathiou2024,HuangCaiWang2025,WangMota2025}
---and a growing literature now translates it into statements about the
ultimate fate of the Universe \cite{AndreiIjjasSteinhardt2022,Gialamas2025}.

This paper asks a different question. Rather than ``what is the fate?'', we
ask: \emph{of the fate verdict, how much is forced by the data, and how much
is donated by the assumptions?} The question has a distinguished qualitative
pedigree---\citet{KraussTurner1999} argued a quarter-century ago that dark
energy severs the link between geometry and destiny, and \citet{KalloshLinde2003}
computed observational doomsday bounds for specific potentials---but it has not, to our
knowledge, been given a quantitative, preregistered treatment in which the
sensitivity of fate probabilities to every analysis choice is itself the
measured quantity.

Our design principle is simple: every conclusion is accompanied by its
derivative with respect to the assumptions. We freeze an analysis plan before
touching real data (Sec.~\ref{sec:prereg}), validate the pipeline against the
official DESI DR2 chains (Sec.~\ref{sec:data}), propagate every posterior
sample to $a=10^{4}$ through a frozen fate classifier (Sec.~\ref{sec:fate}),
calibrate the classifier on one hundred mock universes in which the truth is
exactly \lcdm{} (Sec.~\ref{sec:null}), and then measure the fate verdict's
response along eight axes of assumption variation (Sec.~\ref{sec:matrix}):
the supernova compilation, the SH0ES anchor, the CMB information content,
the dataset combination, the prior on the dark-energy sector, the inference
statistic, the $w(a)$ parametrization, and the position of the truth itself.

Three results organize the paper. First, under the null: when the pipeline is
fed data whose truth is exactly \lcdm{}, the direction of the fate verdict
(dissipation versus rip) is a coin flip---the heat-death-compatible posterior
mass is consistent with a uniform distribution, and even a zero-noise
realization returns $52/48$. This is not a pipeline defect but a theorem-shaped
consequence of the fact that \lcdm{} sits on a zero-width fate boundary.
Second, in depth: the real data push the Big Rip probability to $0.18\%$ under
CPL, eight times below the lowest value reached by any of the hundred null
mocks (calibrated $p<0.030$), converging with the Wilks route ($p=0.024$)---a
genuine, doubly-verified thawing signal. Third, in translation: converting
that signal into a fate statement is dominated by the extrapolation rule.
Across five parametrizations that are indistinguishable inside the data
window~\cite{Wolf2025}, the Big Rip probability moves from zero (a
Gaussian-process grammar whose mean function forbids it) through $0.2\%$
(CPL) to $41\%$ (binned $w$), and the heat-death-compatible probability
from $58.8\%$ to $100\%$;
under a quintessence prior the rip probability is zero before any data
arrive. The fate epoch lies entirely beyond $a=1$, where the
data--posterior information content vanishes identically
(Sec.~\ref{sec:ah}).

We conclude that the current cosmic-fate literature is reporting, with
precision, the echo of its own extrapolation conventions. The constructive
output of this paper is the audit framework itself: a preregistered,
null-calibrated, assumption-differentiated protocol that separates what the
data say (a thawing-quadrant signal at the $2$--$3\sigma$ level, robust to
every dataset ablation we tried) from what the grammar says (essentially
everything about $a>1$).

\section{Preregistered design}\label{sec:prereg}

The analysis plan---model space, priors, dataset combinations, fate-classification
criteria, statistical rules, and pass/fail gates---was frozen under a version-control
tag (\texttt{prereg-v1}) before any real-data fate analysis, and is reproduced
in English translation in Appendix~\ref{app:plan} (the frozen original is
archived verbatim in the repository). Deviations are permitted only through an
append-only amendment log (Appendix~\ref{app:amendments}) recording what changed,
why, and \emph{when} relative to data contact. Three amendments occurred:
A-001 recalibrated the pipeline-validation benchmark after we established that a
compressed-CMB likelihood cannot, by construction, reproduce the full-likelihood
exclusion significance (Sec.~\ref{sec:data}); A-002 replaced a mock-recovery
criterion that is mathematically unsatisfiable for a truth located on a fate
boundary (Sec.~\ref{sec:null}); A-003 added an analytic branch to the fate
criteria after the frozen \textsc{other}-budget audit was triggered by
parametrizations with bounded future $w(a)$ (Sec.~\ref{sec:fparam}). In each
case the trigger, the outcome-independence of the argument, and the sign-off
are logged. One boundary of this discipline should be stated plainly: the
data predate the plan. The DESI DR2 constraints were public before the
freeze, so this preregistration certifies the \emph{integrity of analysis
choices}---criteria, gates, and fragility metrics fixed before any fate
computation, with both possible verdicts (data-dominated or
assumption-dominated fate probabilities) declared equally publishable in
advance---not the novelty of a prediction. The published $\wz$--$\wa$
constraint enters only as the Gate-1 validation target, never as a claim of
this paper; the quantities this paper actually reports (null-calibration
outcomes, cross-grammar fate tables, constraint-horizon values) did not
exist in the literature at freeze time. We additionally registered
falsifiable predictions for the null-calibration campaign before it ran
(six for six correct; Appendix~\ref{app:mocks})---the one component of this
work that was blind in the strict sense. The discipline as a whole is the
single-author analogue of the blinding now institutional in survey
cosmology~\cite{Muir2020,Andrade2025}: where a collaboration shields itself
from confirmation bias by blinding catalogs or data vectors, a single
author can only freeze the analysis choices in advance and anchor the
freeze externally. In a field where the headline significance moves by
$2\sigma$ under defensible analysis variations, we regard this discipline,
not any individual number, as the paper's primary methodological contribution.

\section{Data, likelihoods, and validation}\label{sec:data}

We use three publicly released datasets: DESI DR2 BAO compressed measurements
(13 points over seven redshift bins, $0.295\le z\le 2.33$)~\cite{DESIDR2};
the Pantheon+ Type Ia supernova compilation with full stat+sys covariance,
with and without the SH0ES Cepheid anchor~\cite{Scolnic2022,Brout2022}; and \textit{Planck}
2018 information compressed into the distance priors $(R,\ell_A,\Omega_b h^2)$
of Ref.~\cite{ChenHuangWang2018}, validated there for CPL-type models. Files,
versions, and checksums are registered in the repository manifest
(Appendix~\ref{app:repro}).

Two validation results define what the pipeline can claim. First, against the
official DESI DR2 $w_0w_a$CDM chains, our marginals agree to $\le0.33\sigma$
in every parameter, with $\Omega_m$ and $H_0$ agreeing to $<0.1\sigma$
(Fig.~\ref{fig:gate1}). Second, the compressed CMB prior carries a known,
stable information deficit: our \lcdm{} exclusion is $2.25\sigma$
($\Delta\chi^2=7.44$) where the full-likelihood value is $2.8\sigma$, and the
same $\approx0.5\sigma$ discount recurs across all three supernova
compilations (Sec.~\ref{sec:fdata}), consistent with independent
compressed-likelihood analyses~\cite{CompressedEvidence2025}. Every
significance below inherits the compressed-CMB condition; we price it rather
than hide it. One procedural lesson is worth recording: compressed priors
must be predicted with the same $z_*$/$r_s$ conventions used at extraction---
mixing conventions shifted $\ell_A$ by $3\sigma$ of pure artifact in an early
pipeline version, caught by a known-answer test before any science ran.

\section{Fate taxonomy and classifier}\label{sec:fate}

Each posterior sample defines a background
$H^2(a)/H_0^2=\Omega_r a^{-4}+\Omega_m a^{-3}+\Omega_k a^{-2}
+\Omega_{\rm DE}f_{\rm DE}(a)$, integrated from $a=1$ to $a_{\max}=10^4$ and
classified with frozen criteria, in priority order: \textsc{crunch}
($H^2\le0$ reached); \textsc{rip} (finite-time singularity~\cite{Caldwell2003}: convergent
$\int\!da/aH$; for parametrizations whose $w(a)$ has a finite limit
$w_\infty$, amendment A-003 substitutes the analytic condition
$w_\infty<-1-\epsilon$); \textsc{ds} (de Sitter asymptotics,
$|w+1|<\epsilon$ with persistent dark energy); \textsc{decay} (dark-energy
dilution); \textsc{other} (unclassifiable---audited under a frozen 1\% budget
whose breach triggers the amendment process, as occurred for A-003). Every
class carries a boundary flag: samples that flip when thresholds are doubled
or halved are reported as such.

The taxonomy is deliberately mapped to thermodynamics: \textsc{ds} \emph{and}
\textsc{decay} are both heat-death-compatible (eternal expansion)---a point on
which parts of the recent fate literature are imprecise. \emph{Decaying dark
energy does not rescue the Universe from heat death}; in flat CPL cosmologies
the only non-heat-death outcome is the Big Rip. The classifier passes an
eight-case unit suite spanning all classes, boundary behavior, and two
documented numerical edge cases (Appendix~\ref{app:classifier}).

Figure~\ref{fig:partition} shows the D0 (DESI DR2 + CMB prior + Pantheon+SH0ES)

drawn over the fate partition of the $(\wz,\wa)$ plane. The posterior presses
against the zero-width \textsc{rip}/\textsc{decay} boundary at $\wa=0$; the

Two seeds of unease belong here. First, the same data give
$\ln B(\mathrm{CPL}/\lcdm)=-1.76\pm0.31$: Bayesian evidence \emph{favors}

before any photon arrived. Before interpreting Table~\ref{tab:d0fate} at all,
however, one must ask what such a table looks like when the truth is exactly
\lcdm{}---the subject of the next section.

\section{Null calibration}\label{sec:null}

No fate verdict in the literature has, to our knowledge, been calibrated
against a null. We generated one hundred mock datasets whose truth is the
\lcdm{} best fit to D0, drawing noise from the exact likelihood covariances
(SN $1657\times1657$; BAO $13\times13$; CMB prior $3\times3$), plus one
zero-noise realization for which all three $\chi^2$ vanish identically at the
truth---a closed-loop validation of the mock machinery. Each mock was
processed by the full CPL+P1 pipeline.

Because the \lcdm{} truth sits on the zero-width fate boundary, the
probability integral transform predicts the heat-death-compatible posterior
mass to be distributed as $U(0,1)$ across realizations, hence a direction
verdict correct in $\sim50\%$ of mocks \emph{regardless of pipeline quality}.
(The general statement, its closed-form verification, the diagnostics that
detect a miscalibrated pipeline, and the finite-mock reporting rules are
collected in Appendix~\ref{app:boundarystats}.)
These predictions were registered before the campaign ran. Results
(Fig.~\ref{fig:null}): accuracy $50/100$ (68\% CI $[45,55]$); KS uniformity
$p=0.25$; mean $0.525\pm0.030$; \textsc{other} and boundary budgets at the
$10^{-3}$ level. The zero-noise mock returns $52/48$: even noiseless \lcdm{}
data flip a coin, establishing that the verdict's randomness originates in
boundary geometry, not measurement noise. No calibrated Bayesian procedure
can do better; a pipeline scoring far above 50\% here would be exhibiting
prior bias, not skill.

The null distribution also supplies the calibrated reading of the real data.
Across one hundred \lcdm{} universes the lowest Big Rip probability produced
by noise is $1.52\%$; the real Universe sits at $0.18\%$---eight times deeper,
below every realization (calibrated $p<0.030$), converging with the
Wilks~\cite{Wilks1938} route ($p=0.024$) through an independent argument. The correct summary of
Table~\ref{tab:d0fate} is therefore two-layered: \emph{the direction of the
fate verdict carries no information---even perfect \lcdm{} data flip a
coin---but the depth of the real data's anti-rip preference is genuine,
doubly verified, and corresponds to the $2$--$3\sigma$ thawing signal.}
What that signal does and does not imply about the actual fate is the
subject of the fragility matrix.

\section{The fragility matrix}\label{sec:matrix}
\subsection{Data axis}\label{sec:fdata}

Table~\ref{tab:loo} and Fig.~\ref{fig:loo} show the leave-one-out response. The thawing direction
($\wz>-1$, $\wa<0$) survives every ablation---including removal of the CMB
(D2; the CMB prior is replaced by a BBN baryon-density
prior~\cite{Schoeneberg2024}), which \emph{deepens} rather than erases the preference, a point against
the hypothesis that the evidence is purely CMB--low-$z$ tension. The
magnitude, by contrast, is fragile: $\wa$ swings threefold ($-0.55$ to
$-1.72$), and the full combination is the \emph{most moderate} of all subsets
---the widely quoted $\wa\approx-0.6$ is a negotiated settlement among
datasets pulling with different strengths. Swapping the supernova compilation
(same model, same priors, same BAO and CMB) moves the \lcdm{} exclusion from
$2.25\sigma$ (Pantheon+) through $2.81\sigma$ (DES-Dovekie) to $3.27\sigma$
(Union3), reproducing the literature's significance
ladder~\cite{Efstathiou2024,HuangCaiWang2025} inside a single
pipeline; the SH0ES anchor alone moves $H_0$ by $+1.35$~km\,s$^{-1}$\,Mpc$^{-1}$ and $\wz$ by $-0.034$
---Hubble-tension leakage into the dark-energy sector. Throughout,
$P(\textsc{rip})$ stays within $0$--$0.3\%$: \emph{fate probabilities are
robust to which data one believes} (they are not robust to which grammar one
believes; Sec.~\ref{sec:fparam}).

\subsection{Prior axis}\label{sec:fprior}

Under the physical priors the question dissolves before data arrive: P2
(pointwise $w(a)\ge-1$, quintessence) and P3 (P2 + thawing) force
$P(\textsc{rip})=0$ \emph{identically}---reported, per the frozen plan, as
``excluded by prior.'' The posterior is pinned into a sliver against the
$w=-1$ wall ($\wz=-0.975$, $\wa=-0.015$), and the wall costs the fit
$\Delta\chi^2=+8.9$ relative to P1: a clean measure of the current tension
between the data and the quintessence hypothesis. P3 adds nothing to P2 on
these data---its extra thawing assumptions are redundant once the pointwise
bound binds. The prior axis reading is therefore maximal:
$\log_{10}P(\textsc{rip})$ spans from $-2.7$ to $-\infty$.

\subsection{Inference-statistic axis}\label{sec:fstat}

The same data, model, and priors yield $\Delta\chi^2=7.44$ (Wilks
$p=0.024$, disfavoring \lcdm) and $\ln B=-1.76\pm0.31$ (Jeffreys-scale
support \emph{for} \lcdm). Nothing is inconsistent: the two statistics ask
different questions, and the Occam penalty of the $15.5$-unit $(\wz,\wa)$
prior area dominates the fit improvement. But the practical consequence is
stark---\emph{whether ``the evidence favors dynamical dark energy'' is
true depends on which inference statistic one worships}, and the Bayes
factor's first-order dependence on prior volume makes it a fragility axis of
its own, rarely reported as such.

\subsection{Parametrization axis}\label{sec:fparam}

Table~\ref{tab:grammar} and Fig.~\ref{fig:grammar} are the paper's central
result. Four parametrizations, statistically indistinguishable inside the
data window, are fit to identical data under identical priors; their fate
tables disagree catastrophically. That in-window equivalence is not
peculiar to our pipeline---it has been established independently across a
similar grammar family~\cite{Wolf2025}; this axis measures what the
equivalence conceals beyond the window, where the fate lives.
The divergent-future family (CPL, JBP~\cite{JBP2005}) delivers near-certain \textsc{decay};
the bounded-future family (BA~\cite{BarbozaAlcaniz2008}) revives a small but nonzero \textsc{ds}; the
frozen-future family (BIN4---the most extrapolation-averse grammar, ``the
future keeps the equation of state we measure today'') returns a coin flip:
$41.2\%$ \textsc{rip}, $41.2\%$ \textsc{decay}, $17.6\%$ \textsc{ds}, because
the fate-controlling bin is measured to be $w_1=-1.000\pm0.044$---the real
data land the knife edge of Sec.~\ref{sec:null} at the grammar level. (A
quarter of BIN4 samples carry boundary flags; the three-way split is
threshold-sensitive, which is itself the point.) Meanwhile the binned
reconstruction relocates the thawing signal: the low-$z$ bin is
\lcdm-consistent, and the deviation lives at $z\in[0.7,1.5]$
($w_3=-1.53\pm0.20$)---CPL's famous $\wz\approx-0.88$ is that mid-$z$ signal
transported to $z=0$ along a straight line.

The fifth registered grammar closes the family from the opposite side. We
realize the plan's Gaussian-process reconstruction as six nodes over
$z\in[0,3]$, interpolated by the conditional mean of a squared-exponential
GP with hierarchically sampled $(\sigma_f,\ell)$ and mean function $w=-1$;
outside the data window---in \emph{both} directions---the reconstruction
reverts to its mean by construction. The consequence is exact:
$w(a\to\infty)=-1$ for every posterior sample, so the A-003 asymptotic
criteria return $P(\textsc{ds})=100\%$ with zero Monte Carlo error,
independently of what the data say about the nodes. This is the mirror
image of the quintessence prior (Sec.~\ref{sec:fprior}): there the prior
forbids the rip before any data arrive; here the grammar's mean function
simply \emph{is} the fate. (Assigning each sample its conditional-mean
future is itself a convention; propagating the kernel's future fluctuations
instead would make the verdict a property of the noise ensemble---an
arbitrariness of yet another kind.) The axis reading:
$P(\textsc{rip})$ spans $0$ (grammar-excluded) through $0.2\%$ to $41\%$
($2.3$ dex over the measured range); the heat-death-compatible headline,
robust on every other axis, ranges over $58.8$--$100\%$.

\subsection{Constraint horizon}\label{sec:ah}

Where do the data stop speaking? In the binned grammar the posterior-to-prior
information $D_{\rm KL}[p(w(a))\|\pi]$ is $3.04$, $2.31$, $1.55$, and $0.24$
nat in the four bins (Fig.~\ref{fig:horizon})---the frozen $0.1$-nat horizon
is never crossed inside the window: thanks to the CMB lever arm, the data constrain $w(a)$ (weakly)
all the way to recombination. The horizon statement therefore lands at its
sharpest possible position: \emph{data information terminates at $a=1$}. For

zero; whatever information appears there is transported by the grammar's
extrapolation rule (BIN4's freezing, CPL's straight line), not measured. The
fate verdict depends, in every grammar, on a region where the measured
information content is exactly nil.

\section{Discussion}\label{sec:discussion}

\emph{Three layers.} The audit resolves the fate question into three layers
with different epistemic status. (i) \emph{Direction}: whether dark energy
will decay or rip is, under the null, a coin flip---and under the least
extrapolative grammar, the real data return precisely that coin flip.
(ii) \emph{Depth}: the data contain a genuine signal---a thawing-quadrant
preference of calibrated $p\approx0.02$--$0.03$, doubly verified, located at
intermediate redshift, robust to every dataset ablation. (iii)
\emph{Translation}: converting that signal into eschatology multiplies it by
an extrapolation rule that the data cannot select. The fate literature's
headline numbers live in layer (iii).

\emph{Why the fragility is structural, not accidental.} The fate map is a
\emph{discontinuous} functional of parameters that observation constrains
only \emph{continuously}: \textsc{rip} and \textsc{decay} are separated by a
zero-width boundary, and \lcdm{} sits on it. Finite-precision data can never
settle which side of a measure-zero boundary the truth occupies; if the
cosmological constant is exact, the direction verdict remains a coin flip at
\emph{any} future precision. Improved data can decide the question only if
the truth lies off the boundary---and whether it does is precisely what
cannot be decided from on it. In this specific sense, cosmic fate is either
eventually decidable or permanently undecidable, and we cannot currently
determine which---a sharpened, quantitative instance of the
underdetermination of dark energy articulated in
Ref.~\cite{WolfFerreira2023}.

\emph{What would change the situation.} Not more distance data alone: the
constraint horizon terminates at $a=1$ regardless of precision. Extrapolation
is licensed by trusted theory, not by curve fitting; CPL and its cousins are
conveniences, not theories. A physically derived dark-energy model
(a specific potential, a symmetry argument) would convert fate back into a
derivation---then data would test the theory inside the window and the
theory would carry the license beyond it. Until then, fate probabilities
are measurements of our notation.

\emph{An agent-relevant footnote.} Our taxonomy classes \textsc{ds} and
\textsc{decay} as thermodynamically equivalent (both heat death), but they
differ for the long-term prospects of computation: a de Sitter horizon
imposes a temperature floor that renders total future computation finite
\cite{KraussStarkman2000}, while decaying dark energy reopens the
Dyson regime in which unbounded subjective experience is compatible with
finite free energy \cite{Dyson1979}. On current data the decay direction is
(weakly) preferred---for whatever comfort a $2\sigma$, grammar-conditioned
preference is worth.

\emph{Relation to the recent fate literature.} Papers reporting
$P(\text{fate})$ under a single parametrization or physical
model~\cite{AndreiIjjasSteinhardt2022,Gialamas2025} are reporting real
calculations whose leading uncertainty---the choice of grammar---is absent
from their error budgets. Our Table~\ref{tab:matrix} is the missing error
budget. The framework (preregistration, null calibration, differentiation
along assumption axes) transfers unchanged to any inference whose
conclusion lives beyond its data window.

\emph{Limitations.} All significances inherit the compressed-CMB condition
(priced at $\approx0.5\sigma$; A-001). The analysis is geometry-only; growth
data could shift the window-interior signal. The grammar family, while
spanning divergent, bounded, frozen, and mean-reverting futures, is not
exhaustive---still further parametrizations and reconstructions are in
active use~\cite{Wolf2025}---and our grammar-span
readings are lower bounds. The work was performed by a single author with
AI assistance under the verification protocol described; residual
unknown-unknowns are bounded only by the audit trail, which is public.

\section{Conclusions}\label{sec:conclusions}

The data contain a real signal: a thawing-quadrant preference at calibrated
$p\approx0.02$--$0.03$, located at intermediate redshift, that survives every
dataset ablation we tried. The fate verdict built on it does not: under the
null it is a coin flip, under a quintessence prior it is decided before data
arrive, under a change of inference statistic it reverses, and under a change
of $w(a)$ grammar the Big Rip probability moves by two orders of magnitude
while heat-death compatibility falls from $99.8\%$ to $58.8\%$. The heat
death of the Universe is neither confirmed nor threatened by current data:
every eschatological bit lives beyond $a=1$, where the measured information
content is identically zero. What cosmology can honestly export today is not
a fate but a fate-conditional: the audit framework presented here keeps the
two from being confused.

\section{Frozen analysis plan}\label{app:plan}

The plan below is an English rendering of the document frozen under tag
\texttt{prereg-v1} (2026-07-02, before any real-data fate analysis); the
frozen original is archived in the repository and takes precedence in any
discrepancy. Amendments are in Appendix~\ref{app:amendments}.

\emph{Research question and pre-declared outcomes.} The deliverable is a set
of fragility measures, not a fate discovery. Two outcome forms were declared
in advance as equally publishable: (A) fate probabilities are data-dominated
(variation across priors/parametrizations below one order of magnitude), or
(B) fate probabilities are assumption-dominated (variation of one order of
magnitude or more, or a fate decision falling outside the constraint
horizon). The plan records no preference between them.

\emph{Model.} Background expansion only (no perturbations; growth data out of
scope): $H^2(a)/H_0^2=\Omega_r a^{-4}+\Omega_m a^{-3}+\Omega_k a^{-2}
+\Omega_{\rm DE}f_{\rm DE}(a)$, with $\Omega_r$ fixed from
$T_{\rm CMB}=2.7255$~K and $N_{\rm eff}=3.044$ (massless-neutrino
approximation, recorded as a limitation) and
$\Omega_{\rm DE}=1-\Omega_m-\Omega_k-\Omega_r$ by closure. Baseline
parametrization CPL, $w(a)=\wz+\wa(1-a)$; frozen comparison set for the
parametrization axis: BA, $w(z)=\wz+\wa\,z/(1+z^2)$; JBP,
$w(z)=\wz+\wa\,z/(1+z)^2$; BIN4, constant $w$ in four redshift bins
$z\in[0,0.3)$, $[0.3,0.7)$, $[0.7,1.5)$, $[1.5,z_{\rm CMB}]$, with the
future ($a>1$) inheriting the first bin; and a Gaussian-process
reconstruction, registered as a secondary cross-check (realization and
verdict in Sec.~\ref{sec:fparam}).

\emph{Priors.} P1 (baseline, flat): $\Omega_m\in[0.01,0.99]$;
$H_0\in[20,100]$~km\,s$^{-1}$\,Mpc$^{-1}$; $\Omega_bh^2\in[0.005,0.1]$ when
the CMB prior is used, else Gaussian $0.02218\pm0.00055$ (BBN,
Ref.~\cite{Schoeneberg2024}); $\Omega_k=0$ (baseline); $\wz\in[-3,1]$;
$\wa\in[-3,2]$ with $\wz+\wa<0$ (early matter domination); SN absolute
magnitude $M_B\in[-20,-18]$, anchored by SH0ES when included, analytically
marginalized otherwise. P2 (quintessence): P1 plus the pointwise condition
$w(a)\ge-1$ for all $a\in[a_{\rm CMB},a_{\max}]$. P3 (thawing): P2 plus
$\wa\le0$ and $\wz\ge-1$ (a phenomenological convention, so labeled).

\emph{Data combinations (frozen).} D0 = DESI DR2 BAO + CMB distance prior +
Pantheon+\&SH0ES (baseline); D1 = D0 $-$ SH0ES; D2 = D0 $-$ CMB (BBN prior
instead); D3 = D0 $-$ BAO; D4 = D0 $-$ SN; D5 (optional extension) = D0 with
the SN compilation swapped.

\emph{Fate criteria.} As given in Sec.~\ref{sec:fate}, with numerical
thresholds frozen in Appendix~\ref{app:classifier}, the priority order
\textsc{crunch} $>$ \textsc{rip} $>$ \textsc{ds} $>$ \textsc{decay} $>$
\textsc{other}, the $\times2/\div2$ boundary flag, the 1\%
\textsc{other} audit budget whose breach triggers the amendment process, and
the frozen thermodynamic mapping (\textsc{ds}, \textsc{decay}
$\to$ heat-death-compatible; \textsc{rip}, \textsc{crunch} $\to$
non-heat-death).

\emph{Statistical rules.} MCMC via \texttt{cobaya}; evidence via nested
sampling; convergence $R-1<0.01$ on the worst parameter; nested-model
significance via Wilks $\Delta\chi^2$ with explicit $p$-values; model
comparison via $\Delta$AIC, $\Delta$BIC, and $\ln B$; every fate probability
reported with batch-means Monte Carlo error and boundary-sample fraction;
any tail probability below 1\% must be verified by nested sampling before
entering the main text.

\emph{Fragility measures.} $F_{\rm prior}$, $F_{\rm param}$, $F_{\rm data}$:
the $\log_{10}$ span of $P(\textsc{rip})$ and $P(\text{heat-death})$ across
$\{$P1, P2, P3$\}$, $\{$CPL, BA, JBP, BIN4, GP$\}$, and $\{$D0--D4$\}$
respectively, each with all other choices fixed; a 100-mock null calibration
at the \lcdm{} best fit to D0; and the constraint horizon $a_h$, the scale
factor at which the posterior-to-prior KL divergence of $w(a)$ first drops
below 0.1~nat (BIN4).

\emph{Gates.} Gate~1 (pipeline validation, blocking): reproduce the
published DESI DR2 $w_0w_a$CDM result---best fit inside the published 68\%
contour and \lcdm-exclusion significance within $0.3\sigma$ of the published
$\approx2.8\sigma$ (amended by A-001). Gate~2 (classifier calibration):
$\ge90\%$ of \lcdm-truth mocks classified heat-death-compatible, and
\textsc{other} below 1\% (amended by A-002). Gate~3: all chains entering the
paper satisfy the convergence rule.

\emph{Freeze discipline.} After the \texttt{git} tag, the plan text is
immutable; every change is an append-only amendment entry recording what
changed, why, and whether the trigger arose before or after real-data
contact; exploratory (unplanned) analyses are permitted but must be labeled

\section{Amendment log}\label{app:amendments}

All three amendments are reproduced here in translation; the originals, with
sign-off records, are in the repository.

\emph{A-001 (2026-07-03; during Gate 1, before any fate analysis).}
\textbf{Change:} the Gate-1 significance clause, ``within $0.3\sigma$ of the
literature value ($\approx2.8\sigma$),'' replaced by ``consistent with
published results using a \emph{compressed} CMB likelihood of the same
class'' (Ref.~\cite{CompressedEvidence2025} reports $N_\sigma\approx1.1$--$2.3$
for DESI DR2 + compressed CMB + SN). The best-fit-point clause was unchanged.
\textbf{Reason:} $2.8\sigma$ is a full-\textit{Planck}(+lensing) number; a
three-number distance prior discards lensing and spectral-shape information
and cannot reach it by construction---an information deficit, not a pipeline
error. Evidence: best fit $(\wz,\wa)=(-0.854,-0.520)$ against the official
$(-0.838,-0.62)$, inside the 68\% contour; CMB-prior $\chi^2=1.66$ at the
\textit{Planck} \lcdm{} mean; our $2.25\sigma$ falls inside the published
compressed-likelihood range. \textbf{Consequence:} every significance in
this paper inherits the compressed-CMB condition (priced in
Secs.~\ref{sec:data} and \ref{sec:matrix}).

\emph{A-002 (2026-07-06; predicted during the mock campaign from the
probability-integral-transform argument, confirmed on completion).}
\textbf{Change:} the Gate-2 criterion ``$\ge90\%$ of \lcdm{} mocks judged
heat-death-compatible'' replaced by ``the calibration deliverable is the
null distribution itself; real-data fate statements must be reported in
null-calibrated form.'' The \textsc{other} and boundary budgets were
unchanged (and were met). \textbf{Reason:} the criterion encoded a false
factual presupposition. The \lcdm{} truth sits on the zero-width
\textsc{rip}/\textsc{decay} boundary, so the heat-death-compatible posterior
mass is distributed approximately as $U(0,1)$ under the null and \emph{no}
calibrated Bayesian procedure can exceed $\sim$50\% direction accuracy
(Sec.~\ref{sec:null}). Measured: 50.0\% accuracy, KS $p=0.25$ against
uniformity, zero-noise mock at 52/48, all budgets met, six pre-registered
predictions correct---the failure mode matched the mathematical prediction,
excluding a pipeline-defect explanation. \textbf{Consequence:} Gate 2's
output became a core result of the paper rather than a pass/fail check.

\emph{A-003 (2026-07-06; triggered automatically by the frozen 1\%
\textsc{other} audit during the first parametrization-axis pass).}
\textbf{Change:} an asymptotic branch added to the fate criteria: for
parametrizations whose $w(a)$ has a finite limit $w_\infty$ as
$a\to\infty$, classify analytically---\textsc{rip} iff
$w_\infty<-1-\epsilon$, \textsc{ds} iff $|w_\infty+1|\le\epsilon$ with
persistent dark energy, \textsc{decay} iff $w_\infty>-1+\epsilon$
($\epsilon=0.01$, matching the frozen \textsc{ds} threshold; boundary
flagging unchanged). Divergent-$w$ parametrizations (CPL, JBP) keep the
original numerical criteria. \textbf{Reason:} measured \textsc{other}
fractions of 10.5\% (BA) and 82.4\% (BIN4) breached the budget: bounded or
frozen futures fall into a gap between the numerical criteria (decay slower
than the \textsc{decay} threshold, deviation larger than the \textsc{ds}
threshold, power-law rips converging more slowly than the numerical
\textsc{rip} test at $a_{\max}$). The analytic criterion is exact in these
cases (a constant $w<-1$ future implies a finite-time singularity).
\textbf{Consequence for existing results: zero.} CPL and JBP had
\textsc{other} $=0$; D0--D4, P2/P3, and the entire mock calibration are
CPL-based and numerically unchanged. Reclassification affected BA and BIN4
only (Table~\ref{tab:grammar}).

\section{Classifier numerics and known edges}\label{app:classifier}

The classifier integrates each posterior sample's background from $a=1$ to
$a_{\max}=10^4$ and applies Sec.~\ref{sec:fate}'s criteria with the frozen
thresholds: \textsc{rip} tail-convergence tolerance $10^{-3}$ (relative
change of $t(a)$ from $a_{\max}$ to $2a_{\max}$); \textsc{ds} tolerance
$|w(a_{\max})+1|<0.01$; \textsc{decay} thresholds
$\rho_{\rm DE}(a_{\max})/\rho_{\rm DE}(1)<0.01$ or
$\rho_{\rm DE}/\rho_m(a_{\max})<1$; A-003 asymptotic tolerance
$\epsilon=0.01$. Numerics: the age integral $\int da/(aH)$ is evaluated by
adaptive quadrature on 40 logarithmically spaced panels; the \textsc{crunch}
scan uses a 400-point logarithmic grid in $a$; $\ln f_{\rm DE}$ is capped at
700 before exponentiation to avoid overflow (samples beyond the cap are
already deep in the \textsc{rip} regime). The boundary flag re-runs the
classification with all thresholds doubled and halved and marks any sample
whose label flips.

The implementation passes an eight-case unit suite: \lcdm{}
$\to$ \textsc{ds} (unflagged); phantom $\wa>0$ $\to$ \textsc{rip}; the DR2
best fit $\to$ \textsc{decay}; mild thawing $\to$ \textsc{decay}; a closed,
decaying-DE case $\to$ \textsc{crunch}; $\wz=-1.007$, $\wa=0$
$\to$ \textsc{ds} with the boundary flag correctly raised; and two
\emph{documented limitation cases} of the frozen criteria, constant-$w$
curves with $w=-0.98$ (quasi-$\Lambda$: neither \textsc{ds}-tight nor
decayed at $a_{\max}$) and $w=-1.2$ (power-law phantom: a true finite-time
singularity whose tail convergence at $a_{\max}=10^4$ is slower than the
frozen tolerance), both of which are intentionally routed to \textsc{other},
i.e.\ to the manual-audit channel. Constant-$w$ curves are measure-zero in
the CPL posterior; the posterior mass in the band $|\wa|<3\times10^{-4}$ is
$\sim10^{-3}$, safely inside the 1\% \textsc{other} budget. These edges were
documented before the real-data runs and did not require an amendment; the
distinct gap later exposed by bounded-future grammars did (A-003,
Appendix~\ref{app:amendments}).

\section{Mock machinery and zero-noise validation}\label{app:mocks}

The null-calibration truth is the \lcdm{} best fit to D0
($\Omega_m=0.2968$, $H_0=68.81$, $M_B=-19.397$), located by repeated
bounded minimization. Each of the 100 mocks draws one noise realization per
likelihood from the exact covariances used in inference---SN
$1657\times1657$ (stat+sys, after the $z>0.01$ mask), BAO $13\times13$, CMB
prior $3\times3$---added to the truth's predicted data vectors, and is then
processed by the full CPL+P1 pipeline (MCMC and classifier identical to the
real-data runs). Mocks are generated deterministically from a single seed,
so the entire campaign regenerates bit-identically from the repository. A
101st, zero-noise realization (\texttt{mock000}) provides a closed-loop
test: at the truth point all three likelihood $\chi^2$ vanish identically,
verifying the mock--likelihood round trip; its fate verdict (52/48) is the
noise-free boundary diagnostic of Sec.~\ref{sec:null}.

Six falsifiable predictions were registered before the campaign ran:
direction accuracy $50\pm10\%$; $P_{\rm heat}$ consistent with $U(0,1)$;
0--3 of 100 mocks below the real data's $P(\textsc{rip})$;
\textsc{other} and boundary budgets at the $10^{-3}$ level;
\texttt{mock000} near 0.5; and the resulting A-002 trigger. All six were
correct. Per-mock records (101 rows), summary statistics, and the batch
logs are in the repository.

\section{Boundary calibration statistics}\label{app:boundarystats}

This appendix collects, in model-independent form, the statistical facts
behind the null calibration of Sec.~\ref{sec:null}: the exact uniformity
result, its closed-form verification, the diagnostics that detect a
\emph{mis}calibrated pipeline, the finite-mock reporting rules, and the
relation to the calibration literature.

\emph{One dimension.} Let $\hat{x}\sim\mathcal{N}(\theta_0,\sigma^2)$ with
posterior $\theta\,|\,\hat{x}\sim\mathcal{N}(\hat{x},\sigma^2)$ (the
calibrated case), and let class $A$ be $\theta>0$ with the truth on the
boundary, $\theta_0=0$. Then $P(A\,|\,\hat{x})=\Phi(\hat{x}/\sigma)=\Phi(Z)$
with $Z\sim\mathcal{N}(0,1)$: by the probability integral
transform~\cite{Rosenblatt1952}, the class probability is \emph{exactly}
$U(0,1)$ across realizations. Numerically ($2\times10^5$ replications):
mean $0.5010$, variance $0.08356$ against the uniform value $1/12\approx
0.08333$, direction rate $0.502$, KS $p=0.16$. Because the class
probability is a continuous functional of the data, its null distribution
has no atom at $1/2$: individual realizations can look arbitrarily
confident while the ensemble verdict remains a fair coin.

\emph{Two dimensions, correlated errors.} In a $(u,v)$ analogue of
$(\wz{+}1,\wa)$ with a D0-like degenerate covariance and a linear fate
score $s=\alpha u+v$ ($\alpha=1.2$, class $s>0$), the same argument applies
to any linear boundary through the truth: $5\times10^4$ replications give
mean $0.5013$, variance $0.08326$, direction rate $0.505$, KS $p=0.058$.
The CPL fate boundary $\wa=0$ is exactly linear, so the result covers the
geometry of Sec.~\ref{sec:null} up to the non-Gaussianity of the real
posterior---which the full-pipeline campaign tests directly.

\begin{figure}[t]
\includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{figures/figA_boundary_toy.pdf}
\caption{The boundary-calibration toy (Appendix~\ref{app:boundarystats}).
Left: calibrated posterior with the truth on the boundary---class
probabilities exactly uniform. Center: truth off the boundary by
$0.5\sigma$---direction information appears, as it should. Right:
over-confident posterior---mean preserved but variance above $1/12$; the
uniformity diagnostics fail precisely when the pipeline is defective.}
\label{fig:boundarytoy}
\end{figure}

\emph{Diagnostics: when uniformity must fail.} The value of the null test
lies in what breaks it (Fig.~\ref{fig:boundarytoy}). Truth off the
boundary by $0.5\sigma$: mean $0.639$, direction rate $0.692$, KS
$p\approx0$---direction information appears exactly when it exists.
Decision threshold shifted while the truth sits on it: still uniform (KS
$p=0.25$)---only the truth--boundary distance matters. Posterior too wide
(under-confident, $1.5\sigma$): mean stays at $0.500$ but the variance
collapses to $0.0496<1/12$. Posterior too tight (over-confident,
$0.7\sigma$): variance inflates to $0.1175>1/12$. Strongly curved
boundary: KS $p=2.5\times10^{-6}$---uniformity is a locally-linear
statement (the CPL boundary is exactly linear). The triple observed in
Sec.~\ref{sec:null}---accuracy $\approx50\%$, uniform shape, variance
consistent with $1/12$---is therefore not merely ``consistent with''
calibration: each defect direction is separately detectable, and none
occurred.

\emph{Finite-mock reporting rules (frozen before the campaign).} With $k$
of $n$ mocks at or below the real-data statistic, we report the plus-one
estimate $\hat{p}=(k+1)/(n+1)$~\cite{PhipsonSmyth2010} and, for $k=0$, the
$95\%$ upper bound $1-0.05^{1/n}$; a Monte Carlo $p$-value is never
reported as zero. For $k=0$, $n=100$: $\hat{p}=0.0099$, upper bound
$0.0295$---quoted throughout as $p<0.030$.

\emph{Relation to the calibration literature.} The lineage runs from the
probability integral transform~\cite{Rosenblatt1952} through prequential
calibration~\cite{Dawid1984} and posterior predictive
checking~\cite{Rubin1984,GelmanMengStern1996}. The closest modern relative
is simulation-based calibration~\cite{Talts2018}, which validates posterior
computation via rank uniformity under prior-drawn truths. The present test
differs in both conditioning and target: the truth is \emph{fixed at the
boundary point}, and the calibrated object is a \emph{decision functional
discontinuous there}---the campaign is a null test of the verdict, not of
the posterior machinery (which Gate 1 validates separately). The practical
residue is a reporting discipline matching the three layers of
Sec.~\ref{sec:discussion}: a fate-style claim should state (i) its
direction-layer verdict together with the null distribution of that
verdict, (ii) its depth-layer tail probability under the finite-simulation
rules above, and (iii) the translation-layer conditions---parametrization,
prior, statistic---under which the verdict holds; Table~\ref{tab:matrix}
is the translation layer's error budget.
